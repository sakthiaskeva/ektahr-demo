What is Professional Tax?

Professional tax is a tax levied by several state governments in India on all individuals who earn an income through any medium, including salary, profession, trade, or business. It is a state-level tax, and the income threshold and slab rates vary from one state to another.


Although the term says 'Professional Tax', it applies to all kinds of professions, trades, and employment. The maximum amount of professional tax that can be levied by any state is restricted to ₹2,500 per annum according to Article 276(2) of the Indian Constitution.

Registration Process

Employers and individuals are required to obtain two types of certificates depending on their status:


  • Professional Tax Enrollment Certificate (PTEC): Required by an individual, professional, or company that employs staff. This covers the entity itself.
  • Professional Tax Registration Certificate (PTRC): Required by the employer to deduct professional tax from an employee's salary and deposit it with the government.

Exemptions from Professional Tax

While most earning individuals are liable to pay PT, certain categories are exempted in most states:


  • Senior citizens (typically above 65 years).
  • Members of the Armed Forces (Army, Navy, Air Force).
  • Physically handicapped persons (with more than 40% disability).
  • Parents of children with a permanent disability or mental retardation.
  • Badli workers (temporary workers in certain industries).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Who is responsible for deducting Professional Tax?

In the case of salaried employees, the employer is responsible for deducting PT from the salary and paying it to the state government. For self-employed individuals, they are responsible for paying it themselves.

Can I claim a deduction for PT in Income Tax?

Yes, under Section 16(iii) of the Income Tax Act, 1961, Professional Tax paid to the state government is allowed as a deduction from gross salary income.

What happens if PT is not paid on time?

Non-payment of professional tax attracts penalties and interest. For example, in Maharashtra, late payment can attract interest at 1.25% per month, along with possible fines.
