What Are OKRs?

Objectives and Key Results (OKR) is a goals-setting methodology used by individuals, teams, and organizations to set ambitious and challenging goals with measurable results. OKR is a simple yet powerful tool, as it is helpful for large companies and startups to execute their strategy with focus and alignment.

OKR is a goal management system used to collaborate and achieve goals through a framework that requires check-ins, feedback, continuous learning, and collaboration on a regular basis to identify and solve problems.

What Are the Components of an OKR?

OKR consists of 2 main components:

Objectives

An Objective is basically what is to be achieved. They should be qualitative, time-bound, and inspirational goals so that a team or an individual can execute them. Remember that your objectives must be measurable so that you can regularly track and monitor them.

Key Results

Key Results are benchmarks on how to achieve set objectives. While presenting the objectives, it's necessary to ensure that everyone understands what they are and how to achieve them. Good KRs are specific, realistic, aggressive, and verifiable. They can be set quarterly and evolve as the work progresses.

OKR Examples

For your better understanding, following are some of the common OKRs used by organizations. Refer to them and create your own set of OKRs to improve your organization's functions.

Human Resources OKR Examples

Objective 1

Improve the Internal Hiring Process

  • Reduce average time-to-hire from 45 days to 30 days by the end of Q2.
  • Increase employee referral rate by 20% through structured referral programs.
  • Achieve a candidate offer acceptance rate of 85% or above.
Objective 2

Enhance Employee Engagement and Retention

  • Achieve an Employee Net Promoter Score (eNPS) of 50 or above by year-end.
  • Reduce voluntary attrition rate from 18% to 12% by end of Q4.
  • Conduct 100% of quarterly performance reviews on time.
Objective 3

Optimize Training and Development Programs

  • Increase training completion rate across all departments to 95%.
  • Achieve an average feedback score of 4.5/5 on all internal training sessions.
  • Launch at least 3 new skill-development programs aligned with business goals.

Finance OKR Examples

Objective 1

Improve Financial Reporting Accuracy and Speed

  • Reduce monthly close cycle from 10 days to 5 days by Q3.
  • Achieve 100% on-time financial report submissions for all departments.
  • Reduce financial data errors by 40% through automation tools.
Objective 2

Reduce Overall Operational Costs

  • Identify and eliminate at least ₹20 Lakhs in unnecessary expenditure by Q2.
  • Reduce vendor payment processing time by 30% through workflow automation.
  • Achieve a budget variance of less than 5% across all departments.

Sales OKR Examples

Objective 1

Increase New Customer Acquisition

  • Acquire 150 new paying customers in Q3, up from 100 in Q2.
  • Increase qualified leads generated per month by 35%.
  • Improve lead-to-close conversion rate from 15% to 22%.
Objective 2

Grow Revenue from Existing Accounts

  • Achieve a Net Revenue Retention (NRR) of 120% by year-end.
  • Upsell premium plans to 30% of existing mid-tier customers.
  • Reduce churn rate from 8% to 4% by strengthening account management.

Marketing OKR Examples

Objective 1

Boost Brand Awareness and Online Presence

  • Grow organic website traffic by 50% compared to the same quarter last year.
  • Increase social media followers across platforms by 25%.
  • Publish 12 high-quality SEO-optimized blog articles per month.
Objective 2

Increase Lead Generation through Digital Campaigns

  • Generate 2,000 Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs) per month by Q3.
  • Reduce Cost Per Lead (CPL) from ₹800 to ₹500 through targeted campaigns.
  • Achieve a 5% click-through rate (CTR) on all email marketing campaigns.

Engineering OKR Examples

Objective 1

Improve Product Quality and Reliability

  • Reduce production bugs reported by users by 40% in Q3.
  • Achieve 99.9% system uptime / SLA compliance by end of Q4.
  • Increase automated test coverage from 60% to 85%.
Objective 2

Accelerate Product Delivery Speed

  • Reduce average feature delivery cycle time from 3 weeks to 10 days.
  • Deploy to production at least 4 times per week with zero critical rollbacks.
  • Complete migration to CI/CD pipeline for all active product modules.

Objective 1

Deliver World-Class Customer Support Experience

  • Achieve a Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) score of 90% or above.
  • Reduce average first-response time to support tickets from 4 hours to 1 hour.
  • Resolve 95% of support tickets within the same business day.

Drive Customer Onboarding Success

  • Reduce time-to-value for new customers from 30 days to 14 days.
  • Achieve a 90-day customer retention rate of 95% for all new accounts.
  • Conduct quarterly business reviews (QBRs) with 100% of enterprise accounts.

Launch a Market-Leading Product Feature

  • Ship the AI-powered analytics dashboard to all users by end of Q2.
  • Achieve 70% feature adoption rate within 60 days of launch.
  • Gather a minimum of 500 user feedback responses to inform the next iteration.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is the difference between KPI and OKR?

KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) measure ongoing business performance and health, while OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) are used to set and track ambitious, time-bound goals. KPIs are typically stable metrics; OKRs are change-oriented and reset each cycle (usually quarterly).

2. How often should OKRs be reviewed?

OKRs are typically set quarterly and reviewed weekly or bi-weekly during team check-ins. A final review is done at the end of the quarter to assess performance and plan the next cycle. Annual OKRs for broader company goals are also reviewed quarterly for alignment.

3. How many OKRs should a team have?

It is recommended to set 3 to 5 Objectives per cycle, each with 2 to 4 Key Results. Having too many OKRs dilutes focus. The goal of OKRs is to prioritize the most impactful work, not to capture every activity a team does.

4. What is a good OKR completion score?

A score of 0.6 to 0.7 (or 60%–70%) out of 1.0 is generally considered a good OKR score. A score of 1.0 (100%) may suggest the goals were set too conservatively. OKRs are meant to be stretch goals, and consistent 70% achievement indicates excellent performance.
